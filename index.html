how to use

- enter a logical expression in the box above, choose a magnitude, and click go.
- inputs consist of a combination of letters, logical operators, and parentheses
- valid operators: & (and), | (or), ! or ~ (not), ^ (xor)
- example: A & (B | !C ^ D)
- magnitude refers to the number of variables to filter the expression through. a magnitude of n results in a 2^n x 2^n grid
- inputted variables do not correspond 1 to 1 with variables shown on the grid. Z & N will display the same output as A & B.
